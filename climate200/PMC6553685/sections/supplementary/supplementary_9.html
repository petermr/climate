Worst-case scenario of the coupled socio-climate model. Setting the social parameters to their bound that most favour non-mitigative behaviour, we get no spread of mitigative behaviour within the considered time-frame. This causes the temperature to increase in a manner similar to the RCP 8.5 scenario ( S1 Fig). Fixed parameter values are κ = 0.02, β = 1.5, δ = 1.5, f max = 4, x 0 = 0.01. All other parameter values are drawn from triangular distributions with upper and lower bounds given in S2 Table. (TIFF)
